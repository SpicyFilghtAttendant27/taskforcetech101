CISSP Certification

Overview

The Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP) is one of the most sought-after certifications in the cybersecurity industry. Offered by (ISC)², CISSP validates an individual's expertise in designing, implementing, and managing cybersecurity programs.

Key Domains

  1. Security and Risk Management
  2. Asset Security
  3. Security Architecture and Engineering
  4. Communication and Network Security
  5. Identity and Access Management (IAM)
  6. Security Assessment and Testing
  7. Security Operations
  8. Software Development Security

Why Get CISSP Certified?

  • Proves your knowledge of advanced cybersecurity concepts.
  • Recognized globally by employers and industry professionals.
  • Helps you earn higher salaries and advance your career.

Exam Preparation Tips

  • Understand the eight CISSP domains and how they interrelate.
  • Use official practice exams to get familiar with the exam format.
  • Dedicate consistent time for study and revision.

Exam Details

Format: Computerized Adaptive Testing (CAT)
Questions: 100–150 multiple-choice and advanced innovative questions
Duration: 3 hours
Passing Score: 700/1000
Cost: $749
