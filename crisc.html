Overview

The Certified in Risk and Information Systems Control (CRISC) certification, offered by ISACA, is designed for IT professionals responsible for managing enterprise risk and implementing controls. Earning a CRISC certification demonstrates your expertise in identifying and managing IT risk, and implementing and maintaining information systems controls to mitigate such risk.

Key Domains

The CRISC certification encompasses four primary domains:

  • Governance: Understanding organizational governance and risk management frameworks.
  • IT Risk Assessment: Identifying and assessing IT risk to ensure alignment with the organization's risk appetite and tolerance.
  • Risk Response and Reporting: Developing and implementing risk responses to ensure that risk factors are managed effectively.
  • Information Technology and Security: Overseeing IT operations and cybersecurity measures to safeguard information assets.

Benefits of CRISC Certification

  • Enhances your understanding of enterprise IT risk management.
  • Validates your ability to design and implement controls to mitigate risks.
  • Increases your value to employers and opens up career advancement opportunities.
  • Recognized globally as a mark of excellence in risk and information systems control.

Certification Requirements

To obtain the CRISC certification, candidates must:

  • Pass the CRISC exam, which consists of 150 multiple-choice questions covering the four domains.
  • Have at least three years of cumulative work experience in IT risk management and IS control, across at least two of the four CRISC domains.
  • Submit the CRISC Certification Application within five years of passing the exam.

For detailed information on certification requirements, visit the official ISACA CRISC certification page.

Exam Details

Format 150 multiple-choice questions
Duration 4 hours
Passing Score 450 out of 800
Cost US$575 for ISACA members; US$760 for non-members

Note: Exam content and fees are subject to change. For the most current information, refer to the official ISACA CRISC exam page.

Maintaining Your CRISC Certification

To maintain your CRISC certification, you must:

  • Earn and report a minimum of 20 Continuing Professional Education (CPE) hours annually.
  • Accumulate a total of 120 CPE hours over a three-year reporting period.
  • Pay the annual maintenance fee (US$45 for ISACA members; US$85 for non-members).
  • Comply with ISACA's Code of Professional Ethics and the CPE policy.

For more details on maintaining your certification, visit the ISACA CRISC maintenance page.
